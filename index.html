The Missing Layer in Enterprise AI

AI agents are shipping into real workflows ‚Äì contract review, support resolution, deal approvals ‚Äì but they're hitting a wall that traditional systems can't solve. The problem isn't missing data. It's missing decision traces.

Current systems of record capture what happened, but not why it happened. When a discount gets approved, a deal structure changes, or an exception is granted, the reasoning behind that decision lives in Slack threads, Zoom calls, and people's heads ‚Äì never as durable, searchable data.

‚ùå What's Missing Today

  • Exception Logic: "We always give healthcare companies an extra 10% because their procurement cycles are brutal" ‚Äì tribal knowledge, not captured
  • Precedent: "We structured a similar deal for Company X last quarter" ‚Äì no system links those decisions
  • Cross-System Synthesis: Checking ARR in Salesforce, escalations in Zendesk, and Slack threads ‚Äì synthesis happens in heads, not systems
  • Approval Context: A VP approves a discount on a call ‚Äì the system shows the final price, not who approved or why

‚úì What Context Graphs Enable

  • Decision Lineage: Structured, replayable history of how context turned into action
  • Living Precedent: Past decisions become searchable, preventing re-learning the same edge case every quarter
  • Auditable Autonomy: Understand exactly why an AI agent made a specific decision at decision time
  • Compounding Intelligence: Every decision adds to the graph, making agents smarter over time

This isn't just a better way to build AI systems ‚Äì it's a trillion-dollar opportunity to create entirely new systems of record for the decisions that actually run enterprises.
